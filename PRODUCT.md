# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in Argentina who want to understand Club San Jorge capitalization and savings plans before contacting an agency. They are comparing car, motorcycle, and purchase-order alternatives, usually from a mobile device, and need clear guidance without feeling pushed into a transactional checkout.

## Product Purpose

This site is a commercial and informational layer for Agencias Abed, a Club San Jorge agency. It helps users understand the Plan 330 system, browse available catalog options, review draws and adjudications, access useful resources, and start an assisted inquiry or pre-subscription conversation.

Success means the user can understand what the system is, compare options without confusion, trust the agency as an advisory channel, and contact the agency with more context.

## Brand Personality

Clear, serious, and helpful. The interface should feel modern, sober, commercial, and trustworthy, with enough visual strength to feel more polished than an institutional information page.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages.
- Pricing-table patterns that make plans look like software subscriptions.
- Flashy sales copy that implies automatic vehicle delivery or guaranteed outcomes.
- Defensive legal walls that create fear instead of clarity.
- Template-like grids of identical cards with weak hierarchy.
- Fake confidence language such as empty promises, dream fulfillment cliches, or unsupported leadership claims.

## Design Principles

- Explain first, convert second.
- Keep the official system layer separate from the agency advisory layer.
- Make catalog comparison practical, scannable, and mobile-friendly.
- Use Club San Jorge red and dark gray as the main visual weight, with green as an accent.
- Be precise about Plan 330, draws, adjudications, and assisted inquiry language.
- Prefer calm confidence over hype.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Prioritize mobile-first layouts, readable contrast, keyboard navigation, visible focus states, reduced cognitive load, and clear form states. Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences and should never hide essential content.
